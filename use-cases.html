Use Case Scenarios

Discover how the AI-Powered Incident Learning Assistant actively supports teams across the incident lifecycle — from live response to post-incident learning — enhancing speed, confidence, and organizational knowledge.

1

Live Incident Response Support

SREs and On-Call Engineers

Scenario

An SRE receives a PagerDuty alert about high latency in a critical microservice. They jump into Slack and the CLI to investigate.

Outcome

Faster diagnosis and resolution under pressure, with reduced cognitive load and fewer errors.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Instantly retrieves past incidents with similar log patterns and service context
  • •Suggests effective debugging commands used previously by other team members
  • •Highlights recent configuration changes linked to similar latency spikes
2

Zero-Effort Postmortem Generation

SREs and DevOps Engineers

Scenario

The incident is resolved, but writing a postmortem feels time-consuming and tedious.

Outcome

Accurate, actionable documentation without extra effort, improving learning and compliance.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Compiles a complete timeline of actions, logs, and Slack messages
  • •Identifies likely root causes, contributing factors, and resolution steps
  • •Generates a first-draft postmortem in the team's preferred template
3

Dynamic Runbook Suggestions During Troubleshooting

Junior Developers, On-Call Engineers

Scenario

A junior developer is paged for an error in a legacy service they've never worked on before.

Outcome

Developers gain confidence and effectiveness, reducing escalation risks and downtime.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Detects the affected service and error type
  • •Suggests relevant runbook sections updated during similar past incidents
  • •Highlights previously successful and unsuccessful steps
4

Accelerated Onboarding with Institutional Memory

New Team Members

Scenario

A new engineer joins and needs to ramp up on critical systems quickly.

Outcome

Faster onboarding, reduced knowledge silos, and fewer repeated mistakes.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Presents curated summaries of top incidents affecting their services
  • •Shows past resolutions and lessons learned
  • •Suggests documents, dashboards, and people to follow
5

Systemic Insight for Engineering Managers

Engineering Managers

Scenario

An EM wants to identify trends and allocate resources to reduce downtime effectively.

Outcome

Data-driven decisions, targeted reliability improvements, and defensible resourcing plans.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Highlights incident frequency trends by service, team, and time
  • •Flags services with highest MTTR or recurring failures
  • •Correlates team handovers and deployment schedules with incidents
6

Handling Repeated Issues with Subtle Variations

SREs, Developers

Scenario

A previously resolved bug reappears in a slightly different form, such as a new log message or service version.

Outcome

Teams avoid reinventing solutions and save critical time resolving recurring issues.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Detects patterns using embeddings and knowledge graph relationships
  • •Highlights differences in environment, service version, or configuration
  • •Suggests prior resolution paths while flagging previously failed attempts
7

Cross-Team Collaboration & Handoff Continuity

Multi-Timezone Teams, SREs

Scenario

An incident escalates across teams and time zones with no clear ownership trail.

Outcome

Smoother handoffs, stronger collaboration, and faster final resolution.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Maintains a complete incident memory of prior actions and actors
  • •Summarizes the current state for incoming engineers
  • •Ensures timeline continuity with related documentation
8

Incident Review and Retrospective

Entire Engineering Teams

Scenario

Teams conduct weekly incident reviews to identify what worked and what can be improved.

Outcome

Accelerated team learning, improved reliability, and smarter long-term operations.

How the Assistant Helps

  • •Groups incidents by root cause or service
  • •Visualizes recurring patterns and overlooked solutions
  • •Highlights improvements such as updated runbooks or better detection thresholds
